    • The Big Event

    During The Big Event at A&M, I had the incredible opportunity to participate in a meaningful environmental initiative. Alongside fellow volunteers, we dedicated our day to planting trees and laying at mulch in local College Station/Bryan homes. The sense of accomplishment was profound as we watched those young saplings find their place in the earth, knowing that our efforts would contribute to a greener, healthier town environment.

    TreeFolks

    Working with Treefolks in Austin was an enlightening experience. This non-profit organization is deeply committed to the restoration and preservation of Austin's natural landscape. Together, we planted trees in various urban areas, improving the city's aesthetics and environment. Witnessing the local community's dedication to fostering a greener future was truly inspiring.

    Bee Cave Park

    Volunteering at Bee Cave City Park was a valuable lesson in environmental stewardship. Our group joined forces to pick up trash and debris that had accumulated in my hometown park. With every piece of litter collected, we knew we were helping preserve this special place for future generations to enjoy. It was a reminder of the importance of maintaining and protecting our local parks for the well-being of both nature and our community.
